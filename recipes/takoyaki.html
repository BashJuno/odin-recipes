Takoyaki

What is Takoyaki

Takoyaki - commonly known in English as Japanese octopus balls - is a quintessential Japanese street food that's found especially at summer festivals in Japan. Essentially they're round balls of fluffy dough that are smothered with a special savory takoyaki sauce and have a tasty piece of octopus meat at the center.

Ingredients

  • Dashi-flavored batter
  • Octopus
  • Beni shoga (pickled red ginger)
  • Green onion
  • Tenkasu or Agedama (Tempura scraps)

Preparation

  1. Prepare your octopus if you have purchased it fresh rather than cooked.
  2. Chop up the cooked octopus.
  3. Grind some katsuobushi flakes.
  4. Combine the dry ingredients of the recipe.
  5. Whisk together 2 eggs and 1 teaspoon of soy sauce.
  6. Add the dashi stock slowly, a little at a time.
  7. Heat the takoyaki pan on medium high heat.
  8. Wait until you see smoke rising from the pan before adding the batter.
  9. Add the octopus, green onion, tenkatsu and powdered katsuobushi to the takoyaki.
  10. Set a three minute timer.
  11. Break the connected batter between each bowl after the timer goes off.
  12. Set a timer for 4 minutes.
  13. Transfer the takoyaki to a plate.
